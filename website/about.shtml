About

Mappa Mercia is a project of the Openstreetmap users in the Midlands. Its aim is to digitally remap the West Midlands in a free and open format.

The reason for this is that most maps you think of as free actually have legal or technical restrictions on their use, holding back people from using them in creative, productive, or unexpected ways. Openstreetmap wants to change this by creating free geographic data for everyone.

OpenStreetMap

What Mappa Mercia tries to do for the West Midlands does Openstreetmap for the whole world: Create and provide free geographic data such as street maps to anyone who wants them. Mappa Mercia is one small step in this enormous endeavour. You can find more information about the Openstreetmap project on the Openstreetmap wiki.

Finishing Birmingham

The first goal of Mappa Mercia was to map all roads within the motorway ring around Birmingham. This target was archived on Christmas 2008. A video of all the traces that were collected while mapping the city can be found here: fast version and slow version.

What Does the Name “Mappa Mercia” Mean?

The name Mappa Mercia is derived from two historical names which closely linked with the Midlands: The Hereford Mappa Mundi and the ancient kingdom of Mercia.

Mappa Mundi is a general name for Medievial European maps of the world and the largest preserved of these maps happens to be the Hereford Mappa Mundi from around 1300 which is on display in the Hereford Cathedral.

The kings of Mercia reigned from the 5th to the 9th century over the area that we nowadays call the Midlands. The Offa's Dyke on the border of England and Wales is probably the most famous relict of Mercia.

So, Mappa Mercia basically means a Map of the Midlands. And that's what we want to create!
