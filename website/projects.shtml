Mapping the Canals

Birmingham claims to have more canals then Venice. Most of the canals are already on the map. However there are plenty of features on and along the watersways that are still missing from map. Many locks, moorings, towpaths and access points are waiting to be mapped.

Mapping the University Campuses

There are three universities in Birmingham. At the moment their campuses are mapped relatively incomplete. Especially the buildings are not yet on the map. The mapping of the Birmingham unis is coordinated on this wiki page.

Adding all Blue Plaques

The Birmingham Civic Society is erecting Blue Plaques in the honour of famous Birmingham citizens. It would be really nice to have them all on the map. A list of all plaques is can be found on the society's website.

Mapping and Improving Major Buildings

The map of the city centre features already many buildings. But there are more and those that are in the database can be improved. Have a go at your favourite landmark building!

Mapping Footpaths

Birmingham is covered with footpaths. Having them on the map would make it an invaluable tool for every walker and cyclist in Birmingham!

Adding Bus Routes and Bus Stops

Many bus stops and bus routes still need to be added to the map. Have a look at the Public transport map of Vienna to see what can be done with public transport information in Openstreetmap.

Entering Turn Restrictions

If you want to use maps from Openstreetmap on your sat nav you will want to have the turning restrictions for all junctions in the data. On this site you can find a map that shows the turning restrictions that are currently in the database. So, you easily check (and, of course, add) the restrictions for your favourite junction!

Adding Postboxes

Under the freedom of information act the Royal Mail has provided Openstreetmap with a list of all their postboxes. Sadly, the locations are quite inaccurate so that all postboxes still need to be located exactly before the can be placed on the map. The list from Royal Mail can be accessed on this website. It also shows which postboxes have already been located.

Adding Powerlines

Powerlines are probably not the most exiting thing in the world (unless you touch them, of course!). However, the look nice on the map and it can be fun to find out which ways our electricity takes. Last but not least it is a great opportunity to get your compass out and take some old-school bearings (Though, you still need a gps device to find out where you are since you do not now yet where the power pylon is).

Mapping Redditch or Bromsgrove

Bromsgrove and Redditch are in great parts not yet mapped. This provides a great opportunity to go on some fully fletched mapping adventure. At the same time the size of both places let the job look managable!

Mapping the Black Country

Birmingham would not be without the Black Country. So, why stop at the motorway ring? The Black Country has many roads to be mapped!
